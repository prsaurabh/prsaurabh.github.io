1.Adoption: survey hype vs telemetry truth

The headline numbers contradict each other, and that contradiction is the finding:

↑

What surveys of AI-forward orgs say

LangChain’s State of Agent Engineering (1,340 respondents): 57% have agents in production. An OutSystems survey claims 96% — but only 12% say they can govern them. Treat the high number as definition inflation. LangChain SoAE 2026; OutSystems 2026

↓

What platform telemetry shows

Databricks (usage data from 20,000+ orgs, >60% of the Fortune 500): only ~19% have actually deployed agents. Datadog sees agent-framework adoption roughly doubling YoY — from ~9% to ~18% of orgs. Usage, not claims. Databricks / Datadog State of AI Engineering 2026

◐

What the neutral middle says

McKinsey: 23% scaling an agentic system somewhere, but in any single business function no more than ~10% report scaling. BCG: ~35% of firms are “scalers” getting value, ~60% laggards. McKinsey State of AI; BCG AI Radar Jan 2026

✕

What fails

Gartner: >40% of agentic projects canceled by end-2027. MIT: ~95% of GenAI pilots show no P&L impact. S&P Global: orgs abandoning most AI initiatives jumped 17% → 42% YoY. Gartner Jun 2025; MIT NANDA Aug 2025; S&P Global Oct 2025

Dominant use cases (LangChain 2026): customer service ~27%, research/data analysis ~24%, internal workflow automation ~18%. Coding is the runaway revenue category — Claude Code passed a $2.5B run-rate (Feb 2026), Cursor $2.0B+, Cognition (Devin) $492M at a $26B valuation (May 2026).

Companies land in one of three camps — and increasingly a fourth that blends them:

The build-vs-buy break-even, in one number: a 2026 TCO analysis puts it near ~1M agent conversations/year — buy below ~500K/yr, build above ~2M/yr. And on architecture: multi-agent workflows grew 327% in ~4 months by Databricks’ telemetry, but most deployed systems are still a single agent with tools plus routing. Multi-agent is where new spend goes, not yet the installed base — exactly the Topic 5 lesson, confirmed by usage data.

4.Results — and the honest counterpoint

The claimed wins are real but selectively reported. Hold each against its counter-evidence:

The measurement gap. METR’s randomized trial found experienced open-source maintainers were 19% slower with AI tools while believing they were 20% faster — the single most important humbling result in the field (METR now says uplift has likely improved since and is redesigning the study). And vendor self-resolution rates (Salesforce 83%, Fin ~65%) consistently run 15–30 points above customer-published case studies (Fin 42–50%). Believe the deployment metric, not the marketing metric. METR Jul 2025 / Feb 2026; vendor vs case-study data
Klarna, the canonical over-rotation: its AI assistant did the “work of 700–850 agents” and saved ~$60M/yr — then Klarna partially reversed in 2025, rehiring humans for disputes, fraud, and hardship after quality dropped on complex cases. The lesson every regulated firm now cites: automate the routine lane, keep humans on the compliance-sensitive one. Forbes; CX Dive 2025
What actually predicts success: not model choice — quality gates. Databricks telemetry: orgs using AI governance put 12× more projects into production; orgs using eval tools put 6× more. This is Topics 7–8 of this course, validated at population scale. Databricks 2026

5.How companies organize around it

1

Platform teams own the substrate

A dedicated AI-platform-engineering team owns orchestration, model routing, and runtime governance for all product teams — Thomson Reuters’ Platform Engineering group building its AgentCore hub is the pattern in the wild. “AI engineer” is the fastest-growing US job title two years running; agentic postings up ~280% YoY at ~$185K–$320K base.

2

Evals are standard, not yet mature

89% of agent builders have observability, but only ~25% do both offline and online evals. Quality is the top production blocker (32%); at large enterprises security is the #1 concern (25%).

3

Governance is the bottleneck — and mostly missing

81% of teams are past planning but only 14% have full security sign-off. 88% of orgs had a confirmed or suspected agent security incident in the past year; two-thirds can’t tell agent from human actions in their logs. Fleets are outrunning control: ~38% of orgs now run 100+ agents. Gravitee / Okta / CSA 2026

4

How regulated firms get to “yes”

Architectural containment, not trust: Wells Fargo’s zero-PII-to-LLM design, Cox’s 17 agents under granular managed identity, Google’s human-review-before-merge rule, Klarna putting humans back on the sensitive lanes. Every one of these is a Topic 6/9 defense, deployed for real.

6.Try it — read the numbers like an engineer

Exercise · ~10 minutes, thinking
  1. Practice the survey-vs-telemetry reflex: next time you read “X% of companies use AI agents,” ask two questions — who was sampled (AI-forward self-selectors or a neutral panel?) and self-report or usage data? The honest number is almost always the telemetry one.
  2. Place your own posture: if you shipped the resume agent as a product, which of the four postures (section 2) fits at 10K conversations/year? (Buy — you’re far below the ~1M break-even.)
  3. Discount a claim: you read “our agent resolves 80% of tickets.” What’s your mental adjustment, and why? (Knock 15–30 points off for the vendor-vs-deployment gap; ask what counts as “resolved.”)
  4. Connect it: which single practice, per Databricks, most raises the odds a company’s agents reach production? (Governance and evals — the boring Topics 7–8 work, worth 6–12×.)
